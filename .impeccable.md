## Design Context

### Users
Italian health-conscious consumers (25-45) discovering premium supplements through social media ads (TikTok, Instagram). They arrive from short-form video content on mobile, often late evening. They're curious about longevity, biohacking, and optimizing sleep/energy/focus but overwhelmed by the supplement market. They want someone they trust to curate the best for them.

### Brand Personality
**Curated, Authoritative, Warm** — like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a supplement expert. Not clinical or cold. Not hype-driven or aggressive. The confidence of knowing you've selected the best, combined with the warmth of Italian craftsmanship.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark luxury with warm amber accents — inspired by the amber glow of supplement capsules, Italian evenings, and premium wellness spaces
- **Theme**: Dark mode — users arrive from social media at night on mobile
- **References**: Ferrari (Italian luxury, chiaroscuro editorial), Superhuman (dark premium, confident restraint), Healf (UK supplement curation platform)
- **Anti-references**: Generic supplement stores (iHerb, Amazon listings), clinical/medical aesthetics, neon biohacking vibes
- **4 pillars**: Sleep (purple), Energy (teal), Longevity (green), Focus (blue) — each with its own color identity

### Design Principles
1. **Confident restraint** — Every element earns its place. Premium means less, not more.
2. **Warm darkness** — Dark surfaces tinted toward amber/brown, never pure black or cold gray.
3. **One dramatic gesture per section** — Like Ferrari's use of red: the amber accent should appear sparingly for maximum impact.
4. **Editorial, not e-commerce** — Present supplements like a curated magazine, not a product catalog.
5. **Mobile-first from social** — Most traffic arrives from social ads on phones. The experience must feel native to that context.
